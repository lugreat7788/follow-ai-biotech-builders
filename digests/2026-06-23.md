## AI 制药公司动态 / AI Pharma Company News

**Insilico Medicine (Alex Zhavoronkov)** — 在 BIO 2026 国际大会上宣布与韩国 SK Biopharmaceuticals 达成 AI 药物发现合作，聚焦神经炎症、神经退行性疾病及罕见神经系统疾病等中枢神经系统适应症，潜在总交易金额超 25 亿美元（含 1800 万美元首付款、开发/监管/商业化里程碑款及销售分成），是 Insilico 迄今与亚太企业达成的最大交易。公司称其 Pharma.AI 平台可将「靶点到临床前候选药物」周期压缩至 12–18 个月（行业平均 2.5–4 年）。/ Announced at the BIO 2026 International Convention an AI drug discovery collaboration with South Korea's SK Biopharmaceuticals targeting neuroinflammatory, neurodegenerative, and rare neurological CNS disorders, with a total potential deal value exceeding $2.5 billion (including an $18M upfront payment, development/regulatory/commercial milestones, and royalties) — Insilico's largest deal to date with an Asia-Pacific partner. The company says its Pharma.AI platform compresses target-to-preclinical-candidate timelines to 12–18 months versus the industry's typical 2.5–4 years.

## 行业观点 / Notable Commentary

**Demis Hassabis** — 6月18日在斯坦福商学院与校长 Jonathan Levin 对话，称我们正处于「奇点的山麓」（AI 发展速度约为工业革命的10倍），并呼吁未来5–10年加强国际 AI 监管协调；谈及将 AlphaFold 开源而非商业化的决定时，他表示蛋白质结构这一「基础科学层」本可耗费一万名博士之力才能完成，不应被商业化垄断，而应延续晶体学数据 50 年来开放共享的传统以最大化全球科学效益。/ In a June 18 conversation with Stanford President Jonathan Levin at Stanford GSB, Hassabis said we're in the "foothills of the singularity" (AI progress moving roughly 10x faster than the Industrial Revolution) and called for greater international coordination on AI regulation over the next 5–10 years. On the decision to open-source rather than commercialize AlphaFold, he said the "fundamental science layer" — work that would have taken 10,000 PhDs by hand — shouldn't be commercialized, continuing the field's 50-year tradition of openly sharing crystallography data to maximize global scientific benefit.
